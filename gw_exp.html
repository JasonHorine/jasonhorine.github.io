Good Whiskers

The Good Whiskers site is a crowd-sourced forum for beard-grooming products. The site is my team's deliverable for an assignment to create and depoly a full-stack Rails application in about 4.5 days' coding. This was week six in General Assembly Web Development Immersive course.

    Requirements of the assignment:

  • Have at least 2 models (more if they make sense) â€“ one representing someone using your application, and one that represents the main functional idea for your app
  • Include sign up/log in functionality, with encrypted passwords & an authorization flow
  • Have complete RESTful routes for at least one of your resources with GET, POST, PUT, and DELETE
  • Utilize an ORM (ActiveRecord) to create a database table structure and interact with your relationally-stored data
  • Include wireframes that you designed during the planning process
  • Have semantically clean HTML and CSS
  • Be deployed online and accessible to the public

For about four days' worth of coding, we generally liked what we were able to throw together. In reflection, my team agreed that our biggest shortcoming was underestimating the task of creating a look for the site and setting aside sufficient time for styling.

We had no style guide and it really shows as you navigate around the site. Individual pages were styled differently to personal tastes by each developer. (live and learn!) To see what I did specifically on this project, please click my GitHub link on the home page.
